# The 4-Step Prompt Framework

To get the most relevant and high-quality responses from Gemini, structure your prompts using this proven 4-step framework.

<Card title="The Framework" icon="lightbulb">
1. **Persona**: Who is Gemini acting as?
2. **Task**: What exactly do you need it to do?
3. **Context**: What background information does it need?
4. **Format**: How should the output be structured?
</Card>

## 1. Persona
Give Gemini a role. Setting a persona helps ground the tone and expertise of the response.

*Example: "Act as an expert technical writer..."*

## 2. Task
Clearly define the action you want Gemini to take. Use strong verbs and be specific.

*Example: "...draft a project update email..."*

## 3. Context
Provide the necessary background information or constraints. This prevents Gemini from hallucinating or making incorrect assumptions.

*Example: "...based on the Q3 metrics report attached, highlighting the delay in the launch schedule due to supply chain issues."*

## 4. Format
Specify how you want the information presented. This could be a list, a table, a formal email, or even code.

*Example: "...Format the output as a 3-paragraph email with bullet points for the key metrics."*

<Note>
**Pro Tip**: You don't have to get it right on the first try. Treat your interaction with Gemini as a conversation. If the output isn't quite right, provide feedback and ask it to refine the response based on the new constraints.
</Note>
